Before you start: Work on a clean, flat surface. Touch your metal case or PSU frame periodically to discharge static. Handle PCBs (motherboard, GPU, RAM) by the edges. Keep screws in a small tray or cup.

Tools: #2 Phillips screwdriver, zip ties/Velcro straps, isopropyl wipes or microfiber, thermal paste (if cooler isnâ€™t pre-applied).

Pre-Build Checklist

  • Unbox and inspect all parts; check that standoffs, screws, cables, and brackets are included.
  • Download motherboard manual (PDF) to your phone for reference (front panel pins, M.2 slots, fan headers).
  • Have your OS installer ready (USB for Windows/Linux) and any Wi-Fi/Ethernet access for drivers.
  • Plan airflow: front/bottom = intake, rear/top = exhaust. Aim for slight positive pressure.

Step 1 â€” CPU, RAM & NVMe on the Motherboard (Outside the Case)

  • CPU: Open the socket latch, align triangle markers, place gently, lock lever.
  • RAM: Use the recommended slots (often A2+B2). Press until latches click.
  • NVMe SSD: Insert at an angle, press flat, screw down; apply M.2 heatsink if provided.
  • Cooler backplate: If required, mount the backplate now (easier outside the case).

Step 2 â€” Case Prep & Standoffs

  • Remove both side panels. Install the I/O shield (if separate) into the case.
  • Check that motherboard standoffs match your board size (ATX/mATX/ITX). Move/add as needed.
  • Pre-install front intake and rear/top exhaust fans if the case doesnâ€™t include them.

Step 3 â€” Mount the Motherboard

  • Lower the board into the case, align with I/O shield and standoffs, then screw down in a cross pattern.
  • Connect front-panel cables later (power switch, reset, LEDs) â€” note the pin diagram in the manual.

Step 4 â€” Power Supply (PSU) & Main Cables

  • Install the PSU (fan facing case vent if applicable). Use the included screws.
  • Pre-route: 24-pin ATX, 8-pin (4+4) CPU EPS, PCIe/12VHPWR for GPU, SATA power for drives/fans.
  • Leave ~30% wattage headroom for future upgrades; avoid cable tension or sharp bends.

  • Mount the cooler evenly (tighten in a cross pattern). Plug the fan into CPU_FAN header.
  • For AIO: mount radiator (top or front), observe tube orientation, connect pump header (PUMP/AIO_PUMP).

  • Connect front-panel: PWR SW, RESET, HDD/POWER LED (polarity matters on LEDs).
  • Front USB (3.0/3.2) and front audio headers; consult the manual for locations.
  • Case fans into SYS_FAN headers or hub; plan intake/exhaust direction.

Step 8 â€” Install the GPU

  • Remove the correct PCIe slot covers, seat the GPU into the top x16 slot until it clicks.
  • Screw the bracket, connect PCIe/12VHPWR power firmly; avoid cable sag or stress.
  • Check clearance with side panel and front fans/radiator before closing the case.

Cable Management â€” Quick Wins

  • Route the thick 24-pin and EPS cables behind the motherboard tray; use the nearest grommets.
  • Bunch extra cable length in the PSU shroud area; secure with Velcro/zip ties.
  • Keep cables off fan paths and away from the CPU cooler area for better airflow.
  • Do a final tie-down after your first successful boot â€” you may re-route one or two cables.

First Boot & Essential BIOS Settings

  • Connect monitor, keyboard, and power. Flip PSU switch on, press the case power button.
  • Enter BIOS/UEFI (usually Del or F2).
  • Enable XMP/EXPO for RAM speed; set boot order to your OS USB.
  • Check CPU temps (idle), confirm storage detection (NVMe/SATA drives), and update BIOS if recommended.
  • Save & exit, install OS, then chipset/GPU/LAN/Wi-Fi drivers.

No Display or No Boot? Quick Fixes

  • Reseat RAM (try one stick in the recommended slot), check the 8-pin CPU power and 24-pin ATX.
  • Ensure GPU is fully clicked in and powered; try motherboard HDMI if using an iGPU.
  • Double-check front-panel power switch pins and the PSU rocker switch (I = on).
  • Clear CMOS (refer to manual) if settings seem stuck; try a bare-minimum boot (CPU+1 RAM+GPU).

Post-Build Checks

  • Run Windows Update (or distro updates) and install the latest GPU drivers.
  • Set a basic fan curve in BIOS or vendor software for quieter operation.

  • Back up your OS drive image once everything is stable.
