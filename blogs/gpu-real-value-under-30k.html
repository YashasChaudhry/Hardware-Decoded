GPU Naming Explained: Get the Best Value for Your Money

Cut through marketing names to find the best graphics card for your budget and gaming needs.

GPU naming can feel like a maze of numbers, letters, and brand labels. In 2025, companies like NVIDIA and AMD release cards that sound powerful but aren’t always the best deal. Let’s decode the naming schemes and figure out which models under ₹30,000 actually deliver value.

Understanding the Numbers

Both NVIDIA and AMD use a number system to show the GPU generation and performance tier. Some examples may be from older generations (like 3060 or 6600 XT), but they help explain the system clearly.

  • NVIDIA: RTX 3050, 3060, 4060, etc. → First two digits (30/40) = generation, last two digits (50/60/70) = tier.
  • AMD (older naming): RX 6500 XT, 6600, 7600, etc. → First digit (6/7) = generation, next two digits (500/600) = tier.
  • AMD (new naming): 9070, 9070 XT, etc. → New system avoids confusion with Ryzen CPUs while still indicating generation and tier.
  • Higher tier numbers (50/60/70/80) = better performance, but usually higher price.
  • GPUs like RTX 4060 or RX 7600 are technically entry-level, while 60 Ti, 70, and 70 Ti models are mid-range.

Marketing Traps to Avoid

Some GPUs may look newer but perform worse than older models:

  • “Entry-level” cards like RTX 3050, RX 6500 XT, RTX 4060, or RX 7600 often struggle in modern games.
  • VRAM (Video Memory) matters — 8GB is the minimum for smooth 1080p in 2025.
  • Don’t assume “new generation” always means faster; check benchmarks.

Best Value GPUs Under ₹35K

Based on current pricing and benchmarks, these cards are worth considering:

  • NVIDIA RTX 3060 (12GB): Solid 1080p & decent 1440p performance. Entry-level card.
  • AMD RX 6600 XT (8GB): Excellent for high FPS 1080p gaming. Mid-range card.
  • NVIDIA RTX 4060 (8GB): Newer gen, efficient; technically entry-level, but good for 1080p/1440p.
  • AMD RX 7600 (8GB): Entry-level performance; mid-range models are RX 7600 XT / 7700 series.

Quick Checklist Before Buying

  • ✅ At least 8GB VRAM for modern gaming.
  • ✅ Check power requirements and PSU compatibility.
  • ✅ Compare benchmarks, not just names.
  • ✅ Look for good cooling and warranty support.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Buying a card just because it’s “new.”
  • ❌ Ignoring VRAM size — 4GB is not enough in 2025.
  • ❌ Pairing a powerful GPU with a weak CPU (bottlenecking).

TL;DR: Focus on real-world performance, not just flashy names. Under ₹30K, cards like the RTX 3060, RTX 4060, and RX 6600 XT deliver the best balance of price and power.
