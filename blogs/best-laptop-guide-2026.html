How We Test Laptops at Hardware Decoded

Every laptop in this guide was tested across the same set of tasks and benchmarks so that comparisons are fair and consistent. Here's what we measure:

  • Real-world battery life: Screen at 150 nits, Wi-Fi on, mixed use (browsing, documents, video playback). We don't use manufacturer figures.
  • Performance: Cinebench R24 for CPU; 3DMark Time Spy for GPU; PCMark 10 for real-world productivity.
  • Thermal testing: We measure surface temperatures and fan noise after 30 minutes of sustained CPU load.
  • Display quality: Measured with a colorimeter — colour coverage (sRGB/P3), brightness (nits), contrast ratio, and delta-E colour accuracy.
  • Build quality: Flex testing, keyboard travel and feel, trackpad responsiveness.
  • Gaming (where applicable): Frame rates in Cyberpunk 2077, CS2, and Fortnite at 1080p and 1440p.
  • Port testing: Real-world transfer speeds over USB, Thunderbolt, and SD card slots.

How Much RAM Do You Need in 2026?

RAM is the short-term memory your laptop uses for open apps and tasks. More RAM = smoother multitasking.

  • 8GB — Minimum for basic use. Will feel slow with many tabs open. Avoid if buying new in 2026.
  • 16GB — The sweet spot for most people. Handles everything comfortably for 3–4 years.
  • 32GB — Recommended for power users, programmers, and light creative work.
  • 48GB / 64GB+ — Video editors, engineers, ML developers. The MacBook Pro M4 Max goes up to 128GB.
💡 Key Rule Never buy a laptop with 8GB RAM in 2026 if you plan to keep it for more than 2 years. 16GB is the new minimum.

How Much Storage (SSD) Do You Need?

  • 256GB — Tight. Workable if you store files in the cloud, but you'll feel squeezed.
  • 512GB — Comfortable for most users.
  • 1TB — Recommended for photographers, light video editors, and gamers.
  • 2TB+ — Pro video editors, heavy gamers, large project files.

OLED vs IPS — Which Display Is Better?

OLED displays produce perfect blacks, richer colours, and higher contrast — the image pops in a way IPS simply can't match. They're excellent for creators, movie watchers, and anyone who stares at a screen all day.

IPS displays are brighter in some cases, have no burn-in risk, and are more affordable. For everyday tasks and productivity, a good IPS panel is perfectly fine.

✅ Our Recommendation If you're spending $900+, try to get a laptop with an OLED panel. At $600–$900, a high-quality IPS (400+ nits, good colour coverage) is fine.

Integrated vs Dedicated GPU — Do You Need a Dedicated Graphics Card?

An integrated GPU is built into the processor — it uses system memory and is sufficient for everything except gaming and GPU-intensive creative work.

A dedicated GPU (like an NVIDIA RTX 5060 or 5070) has its own dedicated memory and is far faster for games, 3D rendering, and video effects.

You need a dedicated GPU if: you game, edit video with GPU effects, do 3D modelling, or run machine learning. For everything else, save your money — integrated graphics in 2026 are genuinely good.

Battery Life — What to Expect

Manufacturer battery claims are almost always optimistic. Here are realistic expectations:

  • Apple M4 (Air / Pro): 15–22 hours real-world. The gold standard.
  • Snapdragon X Elite (Surface Laptop 7): 17–20 hours. Best Windows battery life.
  • Intel Core Ultra / AMD Ryzen AI ultrabooks: 10–14 hours typical.
  • Gaming laptops: 5–8 hours light use, 1–3 hours under gaming load.

Port Selection — What Do You Actually Need?

Modern thin laptops sacrifice ports for slimness. Here's what to prioritise:

  • Must have: At least 2× USB-C (preferably Thunderbolt 4). These handle charging, displays, and fast storage.
  • Very useful: USB-A (for older peripherals), HDMI (for monitors without USB-C), SD card slot (for cameras).
  • Avoid: Laptops with only 1 USB-C port — you'll always need a hub, which is annoying.

Should You Care About Upgradeability?

Most modern laptops — especially Apple MacBooks, Dell XPS, and HP Spectre — have RAM and storage soldered directly to the motherboard. You cannot upgrade them later. This means the specs you buy are the specs you're stuck with for the laptop's lifetime.

The main exceptions are the Framework Laptop (fully upgradeable), the Lenovo Legion series (RAM and SSD accessible), and some ThinkPad models. If longevity matters, buy more RAM and storage upfront, or choose an upgradeable laptop.

What is the best laptop for students in 2026?

The best student laptops in 2026 are the MacBook Air 13" M4 ($1,099) for those in the Apple ecosystem, and the Acer Swift 14 AI (~$599) for Windows users on a budget. Both prioritise the two things students need most: long battery life and portability.

Is MacBook or Windows laptop better in 2026?

MacBooks lead on battery life, performance-per-watt, build quality, and the overall Mac software experience. Windows laptops offer more variety, better gaming support, broader enterprise compatibility, and more price options. If you need Windows-specific software or want to game, get a Windows laptop. For everything else — especially if portability and battery matter — MacBook is hard to beat.

How much RAM do I need in a laptop in 2026?

16GB is the minimum we recommend for anyone buying a new laptop in 2026. Students and everyday users can function with 8GB, but will notice slowdowns with many browser tabs and apps open. Creators, programmers, and power users should opt for 32GB.

Is an OLED display worth it in a laptop?

Yes, for most use cases. OLED produces deeper blacks, more vivid colours, and better contrast than standard IPS panels. If you watch movies, edit photos, or simply care about a great-looking screen, OLED is worth the premium. The main downsides are slightly higher cost and the theoretical risk of burn-in over many years of heavy use.

What is Snapdragon X Elite and should I buy a laptop with it?

Snapdragon X Elite is Qualcomm's ARM-based processor for Windows, designed to compete with Apple's M-series chips. It excels at battery life and everyday tasks. App compatibility has improved significantly in 2026 — most mainstream apps run natively. However, some niche software and older programs still run via emulation and may be slower. If your work is Office, browsers, and Teams, Snapdragon X Elite is an excellent choice. For gaming or specialist software, stick with Intel or AMD.

How much storage do I need in a laptop in 2026?

512GB is the comfortable minimum for most users in 2026. If you use cloud storage (iCloud, Google Drive, OneDrive) heavily and don't store large files locally, 256GB is workable. Photographers, video editors, and gamers should look for 1TB or more.

Are gaming laptops good for everyday use?

Yes — modern gaming laptops like the Zephyrus G16 and Legion Slim 5 are capable everyday machines. What is the best laptop for programming in 2026?

The Lenovo ThinkPad X1 Carbon Gen 13 is the top Windows choice for programmers — outstanding keyboard, Linux support, and long battery life. The MacBook Pro M4 is the best overall choice for developers who work with macOS or need maximum sustained CPU performance for compilation and builds.

Should I buy a laptop with a dedicated GPU in 2026?

Only if you game, edit video with GPU effects, do 3D rendering, or run AI/ML tasks. For everyday work, studying, browsing, and document editing, integrated graphics in 2026 are more than enough. Apple's M4 integrated GPU, Intel Arc, and AMD Radeon 890M all handle 4K video playback, light photo editing, and casual gaming fine. A dedicated GPU adds cost, weight, and heat — only pay for it if you'll genuinely use it.

What ports should I look for in a laptop in 2026?

At minimum, look for two USB-C ports (ideally Thunderbolt 4), one USB-A, and an HDMI or SD card slot depending on your needs. Laptops with only one USB-C port force you to always carry a hub — a constant daily annoyance. The ThinkPad X1 Carbon and Lenovo Legion Slim 5 have excellent port selections. Apple MacBooks have Thunderbolt 4 and MagSafe but require a dongle for USB-A and HDMI.
